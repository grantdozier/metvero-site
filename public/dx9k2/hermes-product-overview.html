1 — Introduction
Section 1
Introduction

HERMES, a MetVero product, is the institutional intelligence layer that sits above the sports-organization ecosystem and converts fragmented operational systems into coordinated, real-time decision intelligence.

HERMES is not a GPS platform. It is not an athlete-management system. It is not a scouting tool, a cap or contract tracker, a compliance module, or another analytics dashboard layered on top of tools that already exist.

HERMES is built on a single but powerful premise: a sports organization needs more than data access. It needs institutional context — the ability to understand what is happening across every domain simultaneously, why it is happening, and what action should happen next. HERMES creates that context by organizing information around organizational meaning, not just system structure.

"The organization already has the data. It already has the systems. It already has the staff. What it does not have is the layer that connects those systems into organizational intelligence. HERMES is that layer."

HERMES is domain-agnostic by design. It works for any organization that operates as a complex sporting enterprise — a soccer or football club, an NFL, NBA, or MLB franchise, or a college athletic department. The same intelligence core unifies, interprets, and activates data across the performance, medical, tactical, recruitment, roster, compliance, and commercial domains that define modern sporting operations. HERMES solves the fragmentation problem by sitting above existing systems via API and other approved methods — aggregating and contextualizing information into a single, intelligent operating picture. It does not replace current tools. It makes them work together.

For each deployment, HERMES delivers role-calibrated intelligence to sports science staff, position and assistant coaches, the head coach, the sporting director or general manager, the executive office, and ownership — driving outcomes across competitive performance, financial sustainability, regulatory compliance, and enterprise oversight.

HERMES is built on the same intelligence-abstraction philosophy underlying MetVero's ATHENA platform. Where ATHENA abstracts the university (academic systems → student intelligence), HERMES abstracts the sports organization (performance + medical + recruitment + roster + compliance + commercial systems → organizational intelligence). They share architecture, signal philosophy, and institutional conviction. They serve different operational domains.

2 — Background
Section 2
Background
The Sports Organization Intelligence Gap

A modern sports organization is one of the most complex enterprises in operation. Whether it is a Premier League soccer club, an NFL or NBA franchise, or a Division I athletic department, it simultaneously runs 15 to 25 separate software systems spanning performance, medical, tactical and film, recruitment, roster and cap, compliance, and commercial domains — operating against multi-million- to multi-hundred-million-dollar budgets, in transfer and trade markets that move in hours, under tightening financial regulation.

None of those systems know what the others know.

GPS external load lives in Catapult or STATSports. Recovery and HRV live in Whoop or Oura. Availability and medical clearance live in the AMS — Kitman Labs or Smartabase. Event data lives in Opta or StatsBomb. Scouting lives in Wyscout. Market value lives in TransferRoom. The cap and contract position lives in spreadsheets. Ticketing and fan data live in SeatGeek and Salesforce. Each system manages its domain with genuine capability. None of them interpret across each other.

"Sports organizations do not suffer from a lack of data. They suffer from a lack of institutional visibility. HERMES transforms fragmented operational systems into organizational intelligence."

The result is a problem every sporting director, performance director, and owner recognizes immediately. A sporting director cross-references six systems to evaluate a transfer target — and the window closes before the offer is made. A sports scientist sets training load for the week without knowing that three starters are already flagged for fixture congestion and depressed readiness. An owner learns from the auditor that the squad has breached the Squad Cost Ratio — months after the window to act has closed.

This is not a failure of the systems. It is the ceiling of the era they were built in. Enterprise software was designed to manage activity within its own walls. Interpreting across domains was never part of the design.

The Modern Era Has Made This Worse

The structural fragmentation that has always existed is now compounded by three pressures that organizations are still adjusting to. Transfer and trade windows are measured in hours, not weeks — no manual cross-referencing process can serve at the speed the market now moves. Financial regulation is tightening sharply: the UEFA Squad Cost Ratio falls to 70% of football revenue from 2025/26; the Premier League is replacing PSR with a Squad Cost Ratio framework (85% green / 115% red); pro leagues operate hard caps, soft caps with aprons, and luxury taxes; and college sports has entered a revenue-sharing era with hard rev-share caps. And load and availability have become the highest-ROI margin in sport — keeping high-value players fit is now a financial discipline, not just a medical one.

Organizations that succeed in this environment are not necessarily the ones with the most resources. They are the ones that can make faster, more complete decisions. Decision speed, in the current sporting economy, is the competitive advantage that HERMES directly addresses.

What HERMES Does That No Individual System Can

The data needed to run a sports organization at the highest level already exists. The load metrics, recovery data, availability status, event data, scouting profiles, market values, cap positions, and contract terms are all captured somewhere. The problem is that no system answers the questions that require all of those domains simultaneously — the questions that determine whether an organization wins a transfer, stays inside its financial constraint, protects the availability of its highest-value players, or links a sporting decision to its enterprise outcome.

HERMES answers those questions. Not by replacing the systems that hold the data, but by sitting above them — reading every signal, connecting every domain, and surfacing the cross-domain intelligence that no individual system in Layer 1 can produce.

3.6 The Ontology Engine

The ontology engine is HERMES's deepest differentiator. The competitive moat is not data collection — every system in Layer 1 already collects data. The competitive moat is meaning. HERMES does not store records; it maintains a living model of the organization — every entity, every relationship, every signal, updated in real time.

An ontology is a formal representation of the entities in a domain, the relationships between them, and the meaning of signals in context. HERMES builds and maintains a sports-organization ontology — Athlete, Contract, Availability and Injury Event, Transfer Target, Match, Staff, Signal, and Squad/Roster, and the relationships between them — that enables queries no individual system can answer:

Sample Ontology Query 1: "Which midfielders have an ACWR above 1.4, a soft-tissue availability history in the same muscle group, a contract expiring within 12 months, and a release clause below current market value?" — No individual system contains all those facts. The ontology connects them.

Sample Ontology Query 2: "Which transfer targets have a sporting Fit Score above 80, fit within our remaining Squad-Cost-Ratio headroom, satisfy our work-permit and registration rules, and play a position where we have an expiring contract?" — A recruitment-efficiency question that takes a day across spreadsheets and scouting tools. The ontology answers it in real time.

Sample Ontology Query 3: "What is our projected Squad Cost Ratio next season if we trigger these two contract extensions and our broadcast revenue lands at the mid-case — and at what point does that breach the threshold?" — No cap spreadsheet projects the constraint forward against revenue scenarios. HERMES does.

SOCCER
Football / Soccer Club

Sporting Director model. Tracking is league-provided optical (Second Spectrum, TRACAB) plus club-owned GPS (Catapult Vector, STATSports Apex). The AMS is Kitman Labs or Smartabase. Scouting and market intelligence run on Hudl Wyscout, StatsBomb, and TransferRoom. Talent acquisition is the transfer market, governed by FIFA TMS and the Clearing House.

The constraint is the most complex of any vertical: the UEFA Squad Cost Ratio (70%) plus the Premier League PSR / SCR framework (85% green / 115% red), FIFA transfer governance (ITC, solidarity, training compensation), GBE work permits, and homegrown / locally-trained quotas. Revenue is the three pillars — matchday, broadcast, and commercial.

Constraint: UEFA SCR 70% + PL SCR 85%/115% + FIFA TMS + GBE + homegrown quotas — a polymorphic, multi-layer cost constraint.
NFL
National Football League

General Manager model. Tracking is RFID, not optical — Next Gen Stats via Zebra Technologies chips in shoulder pads and the ball. Film and grading run on PFF and Hudl/XOS. Talent intake is the Draft, Combine, and Pro Days.

The constraint is a HARD salary cap (≈$279.2M in 2025) with signing-bonus proration (max 5 years), void years, dead cap, and the franchise tag — a true ceiling that cannot be exceeded.

Constraint: hard cap (~$279M, 2025) · proration · void years · dead cap · franchise tag.
NBA
National Basketball Association

President of Basketball Operations / GM model. The defining tech signature is a two-vendor split: Sony Hawk-Eye is the official optical tracking layer, and Second Spectrum (Genius Sports) is the official team-analytics layer. Force plates (Hawkin, VALD) and P3 biomechanics feed readiness and pre-draft profiling.

The constraint is a SOFT cap with a luxury tax and escalating first and second aprons (2025-26: cap $154.6M, tax $187.9M, first apron $195.9M, second apron $207.8M). The marquee data/medical issue is load management across an 82-game season and back-to-backs, governed by the league's Player Participation Policy.

Constraint: soft cap + luxury tax + first/second apron. Signature problem: load management across 82 games.
MLB
Major League Baseball

President of Baseball Operations / GM model. Tracking is Statcast (Sony Hawk-Eye optical, since 2020, replacing the TrackMan radar baseline). Pitching biomechanics run on markerless KinaTrax and high-speed Edgertronic cameras.

The constraint is NO hard cap — only a soft deterrent via the Competitive Balance Tax ($237M base in 2025, escalating rates and surcharge tiers). The health signature is distinctive: velocity, spin rate, and release-point drift serve as UCL / Tommy John early-warning signals, detectable weeks before injury.

Constraint: no cap; Competitive Balance Tax ($237M, 2025). Health signature: velocity / spin / release as UCL early-warning.
NCAA
NCAA Division I

Athletic Director model, with an emerging pro-style General Manager. This vertical fuses an academic / eligibility domain (Banner, Workday, SAMS, plus the Academic Progress Rate) on top of the athletic spine — the one domain pro leagues lack, and the explicit bridge to MetVero's ATHENA.

Post-House-settlement (effective July 1, 2025), roster caps replace scholarship limits (football capped at 105), with a ~$20.5M per-school revenue-sharing cap, enforcement by the new College Sports Commission, and NIL managed through INFLCR and Opendorse.

Constraint: rev-share cap (~$20.5M) + roster caps (FB 105) + APR academic eligibility + CSC enforcement.

The Polymorphic Constraint

The constraint slot is the clearest illustration of the abstraction. It is polymorphic across deployments — a hard cap (NFL), a soft cap with aprons (NBA), a luxury tax with no ceiling (MLB), a Squad Cost Ratio (soccer/UEFA), or a revenue-share cap fused with academic eligibility (NCAA). HERMES models the abstract constraint — committed cost versus an allowable envelope, with marginal-impact projection on every new commitment — and binds the concrete rule per deployment. The same is true for the tracking, AMS, scouting, and cap-ledger slots: HERMES models the function, and binds whatever product the organization runs.

6 — The Undeniable Problems
Section 6
The Undeniable Problems
Specific, financially grounded, and recognizable in any sporting organization. These are not pain points — they are institutional exposure.
01
The Transfer / Trade Window Moves in Hours. Cross-System Due Diligence Takes Days.

Evaluating a transfer or trade target requires answers from six different systems: sporting fit from the scouting platform (Wyscout, StatsBomb), market value from TransferRoom or Transfermarkt, availability and injury history from the AMS, contract economics and cap headroom from the ledger, registration and work-permit eligibility from compliance, and tactical fit from event data. Manual cross-referencing takes days.

The targets that change trajectories are decided in hours. Organizations that cannot complete due diligence in time do not make the move — not because the player doesn't fit, but because the information wasn't ready.

Exposure
Missed marquee targets per window translate directly into lost on-pitch value and squad-value upside — a range that scales with the organization's market and budget.
HERMES Resolution
A cross-domain Fit Score — sporting fit + availability history + cap/SCR headroom + contract economics — produced in under 2 hours.
02
Financial-Regulation Exposure Is Discovered After the Fact.

The Squad Cost Ratio, PSR position, or salary-cap state is reconstructed in spreadsheets and surfaces — too late — from the auditor or the league. A breach can mean points deductions, transfer bans, and fines. The marginal impact of a signing on the constraint is rarely modeled before the deal is signed.

Exposure
Points deductions, transfer embargoes, and fines — plus the competitive and commercial cost of a sanction discovered after the corrective window has closed.
HERMES Resolution
Live cap / Squad-Cost-Ratio headroom with marginal-impact modeling on every signing and extension before it is committed.
03
Preventable Soft-Tissue Injuries Drain the Highest-Value Roster Spots.

GPS load lives in Catapult or STATSports. Recovery and HRV live in Whoop, Oura, or Polar. Neuromuscular readiness lives in VALD or Hawkin. Availability and injury history live in the AMS. The fixture calendar lives somewhere else again. The data exists — in systems that do not communicate. Load is set without the full readiness, history, and congestion picture, and avoidable soft-tissue injuries follow.

Exposure
Lost availability of high-value players: direct medical cost, lost on-pitch output, and the depreciation of an asset the organization is paying for whether or not it plays.
HERMES Resolution
Cross-domain load-modification early warning — ACWR + recovery + availability history + fixture congestion — flagged before the session that would cause the injury.
04
Recruitment Spends Heavily Evaluating Players Who Don't Fit.

Sporting fit, financial fit, and registration fit are assessed late and separately. Significant scouting, analyst, and travel investment goes into players who turn out not to fit the wage structure, the cap headroom, the work-permit criteria, or the registration rules — facts that should have been the first filter, not the last.

Exposure
Recruitment investment spent on unviable targets, and opportunity cost against the players who did fit but were evaluated too slowly.
HERMES Resolution
A front-loaded multi-domain Fit Score at the point of evaluation — sporting, financial, medical, and registration fit assessed together, first.
05
The Link Between a Sporting Decision and the Enterprise Outcome Is Invisible Until Reported.

This week's lineup, extension, or transfer decision has a downstream effect on revenue, cost, and enterprise value. That link is reconstructed long after the fact, in board decks and year-end accounts — never available at the moment the decision is made.

Exposure
Capital and roster decisions made without a forward line of sight to their financial and enterprise-value consequences.
HERMES Resolution
A forward-looking projection connecting performance → revenue and cost → enterprise value, available at the executive and ownership lenses.

8 — The Organization Opportunity
Section 8
The AI-Native Operating Model
And the organization opportunity it creates.

Every leap in computing happened when a new layer abstracted the complexity beneath it. Assembly gave way to programming languages. Languages gave way to operating systems. Operating systems gave way to applications. Applications gave way to enterprise systems. Each layer let the people above it stop managing the layer below and start operating at a higher altitude.

Sports organizations have already made one of those leaps. They moved from clipboards and filing cabinets to enterprise systems — athlete-management platforms, GPS, scouting databases, cap tools, ticketing and CRM. Those systems are powerful. But they were built to manage activity within their own walls, not to interpret across them. The leap that has not yet been made is the intelligence layer above them.

The organizations that lead the next decade will not be the ones with the most systems. They will be the ones that made this leap first — the ones that stopped asking their staff to be the integration layer between fifteen tools, and gave them an intelligence layer instead.

"The organization already has the data. It already has the staff. It already has the systems. What it does not have is the layer that connects those systems into organizational intelligence. You are already paying for everything HERMES needs to work. You are not paying for the layer that makes it work together."

The opportunity is not to add another platform to a stack that already has 15 to 25 systems. The opportunity is a more intelligent operating model — one in which athletes receive more coordinated support, staff spend less time on manual reconciliation, the sporting director and executive office have real-time visibility, and ownership makes capital and compliance decisions with confidence rather than latency. HERMES is MetVero's strategic commitment to the organizations that deploy it: not a vendor relationship, but an institutional intelligence partnership.

What HERMES Is Not

HERMES is not a system replacement project. It does not require the organization to migrate off Catapult, the AMS, Wyscout, the cap ledger, or any existing platform. It does not change how staff use the tools they already use. It sits above those systems, reads what they produce, and surfaces the intelligence none of them can produce individually. The only lift required from IT is read-only connection access — APIs, SFTP exports, scheduled reports. HERMES does the rest.

Out-of-Scope Across All Sources

HERMES does not consume any of the following from any source unless expressly approved in writing by the organization and legal counsel:

  • Raw clinical or diagnosis detail beyond availability / clearance status
  • Personal medical records, mental-health records, or treatment notes
  • National ID numbers, passport numbers, or date of birth
  • Individual fan / customer personal data beyond approved aggregates
  • Free-text clinical notes of any kind
  • Banking, payroll-account, or personal financial-account detail
  • Disciplinary or HR records

Retention and Deletion

Default retention: lifetime of engagement + 7 years post-engagement, subject to the organization's records-management policy and GDPR obligations. Final retention schedule confirmed during Discovery. Upon written notice, all consumed source data is deleted within 30 days. HERMES-derived data (Fit Scores, projections, pattern models) is deleted or anonymized at the organization's election. Backup purge rotation: 90 days. Deletion certificates issued upon completion.
