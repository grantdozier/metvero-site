1 — Introduction
Section 1
Introduction

HERMES, a MetVero product, is the institutional intelligence layer that sits above the football program ecosystem and converts fragmented operational systems into coordinated, real-time decision intelligence.

HERMES is not a GPS platform. It is not a compliance software. It is not a recruiting CRM, a film tool, a NIL registry, or another analytics dashboard layered on top of tools that already exist.

HERMES is built on a single but powerful premise: football programs need more than data access. They need institutional context — the ability to understand what is happening across every domain simultaneously, why it is happening, and what action should happen next. HERMES creates that context by organizing information around institutional meaning, not just system structure.

"The football program already has the data. It already has the systems. It already has the staff. What it does not have is a layer that connects those systems into institutional intelligence. HERMES is that layer."

MetVero's intelligence platform is designed to unify, interpret, and activate data across the performance, compliance, recruiting, medical, academic, NIL, and financial domains that define modern football program operations. HERMES solves the fragmentation problem by sitting above existing systems via API and other approved methods — aggregating and contextualizing information into a single, intelligent operating picture. It does not replace current tools. It makes them work together.

For Power 4 programs, HERMES delivers role-calibrated intelligence to sports science staff, position coaches, coordinators, the head coach, the athletic director, and university leadership — driving outcomes across competitive performance, compliance standing, financial sustainability, and institutional oversight.

HERMES is a vertical deployment of the same intelligence abstraction philosophy underlying MetVero's ATHENA platform. Where ATHENA abstracts the university (academic systems → student intelligence), HERMES abstracts the football program (performance + compliance + recruiting + medical + financial systems → program intelligence). They share architecture, signal philosophy, and institutional conviction. They serve different operational domains.

2 — Background
Section 2
Background
The Football Program Intelligence Gap

A Power 4 football program in the current era operates as one of the most complex institutional enterprises in American sport. A single program simultaneously manages 85 scholarship athletes, a 50+ person staff, a $100M+ annual operating budget, NCAA compliance obligations across recruiting, eligibility, and NIL, a transfer portal environment that moves in 48-hour windows, and a performance science infrastructure collecting data from 15 to 25 separate software systems.

None of those systems know what the others know.

Catapult holds GPS workload. Banner holds academic records. The NCAA Compliance Assistant holds eligibility certifications. Sportsware holds medical clearance. INFLCR holds NIL deal registry. On3 holds recruiting evaluations. Hudl holds film. Each system manages its domain with genuine capability. None of them interpret across each other.

"Football programs do not suffer from a lack of data. They suffer from a lack of institutional visibility. HERMES transforms fragmented operational systems into program intelligence."

The result is a problem every athletic director, compliance officer, and head coach recognizes immediately. A recruiting coordinator manually cross-references six systems to evaluate a transfer portal entry — and the window closes before the offer is made. A compliance officer manages 150 NIL deals in a spreadsheet and misses a brand conflict that surfaces only when a dealer calls the AD. A sports science staff has GPS data in one portal and HRV data in another, and makes practice intensity decisions without knowing that three starters have final exams tomorrow and their sleep is compromised. An athletic director discovers the program's APR is approaching the 930 threshold from the NCAA's annual report — 8 months after the academic year ended, when the interventions that could have mattered are 2 years past.

This is not a failure of the systems. It is the ceiling of the era they were built in. Enterprise software was designed to manage activity within its own walls. Interpreting across domains was never part of the design.

The NIL and Transfer Portal Era Has Made This Worse

The structural fragmentation that existed before 2021 has been compounded by two transformations that operating programs are still adjusting to. The NIL era created a compliance layer — brand registries, booster classifications, conflict detection, expiration tracking — that no program system was built to handle. The transfer portal created a recruiting window measured in hours, not weeks, that no manual cross-referencing process can serve at the speed the market moves.

Programs that succeed in this environment are not necessarily the ones with the most resources. They are the ones that can make faster, more complete decisions. Decision speed, in the current football economy, is the competitive advantage that HERMES directly addresses.

What HERMES Does That No Individual System Can

The data needed to run a football program at the highest level exists. The academic records, GPS loads, NIL contracts, eligibility certifications, film tags, scholarship counts, and injury histories are all captured somewhere. The problem is that no system currently answers the questions that require all of those domains simultaneously — the questions that determine whether a program wins in the transfer portal, avoids an NCAA violation, protects its APR, or prevents an avoidable injury.

HERMES answers those questions. Not by replacing the systems that hold the data, but by sitting above them — reading every signal, connecting every domain, and surfacing the cross-domain intelligence that no individual system in Layer 1 can produce.

3.6 The Ontology Engine

The ontology engine is HERMES's deepest differentiator. The competitive moat is not data collection — every system in Layer 1 already collects data. The competitive moat is meaning. HERMES does not store records; it maintains a living institutional model of the program — every entity, every relationship, every signal, updated in real time.

An ontology is a formal representation of the entities in a domain, the relationships between them, and the meaning of signals in context. HERMES builds and maintains a football institution ontology that enables queries no individual system can answer:

Sample Ontology Query 1: "Which defensive linemen have an ACWR above 1.4, are within 9 credits of losing a PTD checkpoint, have a hamstring injury in their history, and have an NIL deal with a performance bonus tied to games played?" — No individual system contains all those facts. The ontology connects them.

Sample Ontology Query 2: "Which recruits in this class have an academic eligibility probability below 70%, represent positions where we have 2+ returning starters, and come from territories where we have no upcoming evaluation scheduled?" — A class construction efficiency question that takes a day from spreadsheets. The ontology answers it in real time.

Sample Ontology Query 3: "What is our projected APR score for next semester if our three most academically at-risk athletes fail to remain enrolled, and at what threshold does that projection trigger bowl ban risk?" — No compliance system projects APR forward in real time. HERMES does.

Section 4
The Six Undeniable Problems
Specific, measurable, financially grounded. These are not pain points — they are institutional exposure.
01
The Transfer Portal Window Is 48 Hours. Your Decision Process Takes 5 Days.

When a player enters the transfer portal, evaluating fit requires answers from six different systems: Banner/Workday for academic records, the NCAA Eligibility Center for eligibility, the NIL collective spreadsheet for budget, the compliance office for visit tracking, the medical staff's EMR for injury history, and the SIS for scholarship counts. Manual cross-referencing takes 3 to 5 business days.

The top 20% of portal entries — the ones that change program trajectories — are decided within 24 to 48 hours. Programs that cannot complete due diligence in under 2 hours do not make offers — not because the player doesn't fit, but because the information wasn't ready in time.

Financial Exposure
3 missed marquee portal targets per cycle = $10M–$30M in institutional value. Single 5-star transfer NIL value: $500K–$2M.
HERMES Resolution
Portal entry triggers automatic cross-domain Fit Score: academic eligibility probability, scholarship slot status, NIL headroom, medical flag summary, and compliance risk — in under 90 minutes.
02
150 Active NIL Deals, Managed on a Spreadsheet.

A mature NIL program has 50 to 65 active deals simultaneously across automotive, food and beverage, apparel, technology, local services, alcohol, gambling, and financial products. Brand conflicts surface only when a dealer calls the AD. Booster entanglement in NIL LLCs surfaces only during an NCAA enforcement review. Contract expirations go untracked until a competing brand calls the player directly.

Financial Exposure
Average Level I NCAA violation: $50K–$100K direct fine + 2–5 scholarship reductions. At $47K/slot/year: $564K in scholarship budget alone over a 4-year compliance period.
HERMES Resolution
Unified NIL ledger with brand category registry, conflict detection engine, booster classification checks, and expiration calendar. Every new deal is reviewed before execution.
03
Scholarship Efficiency Is Bleeding $141,000 to $282,000 Per Year.

Programs lose 3 to 6 scholarship slots annually to fragmented information: missed 14-day medical hardship windows, incorrect graduation transfer departure dates recorded in SIS, and over-signing attrition miscalculations. Each wasted scholarship is worth $47,000 per academic year with zero roster return.

Financial Exposure
3 wasted slots per year = $141K annually. Over a 4-year cycle: $564K in recoverable scholarship efficiency loss.
HERMES Resolution
Real-time scholarship ledger with automated medical hardship deadline tracking, graduation transfer clearance timelines, and roster scenario modeling. Slot mismanagement drops to near zero.
04
Preventable Injuries Are Destroying Game-Week Rosters.

Programs using Catapult GPS collect 15–20 data points per athlete per session. Programs using Polar or Whoop collect HRV and sleep quality. Programs using Hawkin Dynamics collect neuromuscular readiness. The data exists — in three systems that do not communicate. The academic advisor knows that three starters have finals this week and their sleep is disrupted. That information never reaches the sports science staff. A 2022 Power 5 study showed a 34% increase in soft tissue injuries in the two weeks following high-academic-stress periods. The sports science staff makes practice intensity decisions without access to the academic calendar.

Financial Exposure
4–6 preventable soft tissue injuries per season: $500K–$1.2M in direct medical costs, re-recruiting costs, APR impact, and portal departure risk.
HERMES Resolution
Unified load management dashboard: GPS workload + recovery metrics + academic calendar signals simultaneously. ACWR spike + low HRV + exam overlap generates a 48–72 hour early warning flag.
05
Late Academic Disqualification Is Destroying Recruiting Classes.

The academic eligibility check is the final step in the recruiting process. It should be the first. 3 to 5 recruits per class at major programs are discovered — after offer, after official visit, after commitment — to have NCAA core course deficiencies, GPA shortfalls, or test score problems. The offer slot was committed. The class was built around these players. The replacement recruit, signed from what remains in the pool after disqualification, is rarely equivalent.

Financial Exposure
3 late-disqualification recruits per class × $47K scholarship × 4-year participation value = $564K in lost scholarship utilization, plus recruiting investment already spent.
HERMES Resolution
Prospect Fit Score runs academic eligibility modeling at the point of evaluation — before the offer is extended. Programs stop investing heavily in academically unqualifiable prospects.
06
APR Violations Are Invisible Until It's Too Late.

The NCAA APR is a rolling 4-year metric. The 930 threshold triggers sanctions: practice time restrictions, scholarship reductions, and postseason bans. No system currently gives programs a forward-looking APR model. Programs discover they are approaching the 930 threshold when the NCAA publishes the annual report — typically 8 months after the academic year ends. The interventions that could have mattered are 2 years past.

Financial Exposure
SEC bowl ban cost: $4M–$18M in conference distribution + $2M–$5M institutional revenue. Total exposure: $6M–$23M per banned bowl appearance.
HERMES Resolution
Real-time APR projection model per athlete, per semester, projected forward 4–8 semesters. Intervention recommendations surface when rolling trajectory intersects the 930 threshold — with 6 semesters of early warning.

6 — Pro Sports Scalability
Section 6
Pro Sports Scalability
The same architecture. Higher stakes. The identical fragmentation problem at 10x to 50x the financial exposure.

HERMES was designed for the football institution, not for a specific regulatory environment. The ontology architecture, signal taxonomy, and intelligence layers are directly transferable to professional sports operations. The data fragmentation problem is identical. The financial stakes are not.

NFL
National Football League

A 32-team franchise operates with a $255M salary cap (2024), a 53-man active roster, and a $150M–$200M annual operating budget. The NFL equivalent of scholarship management is salary cap management — one incorrect cap restructure produces $10M–$30M in dead cap space. The trade deadline is the transfer portal: evaluating a trade target requires cap impact (OverTheCap), performance signal (PFF, Next Gen Stats), injury history (NFLPA summary), and scheme fit (film) — from at least 6 systems, in 48 hours.

Dead cap mismanagement: $10M–$30M per incident. Draft pick misallocation ($10M–$30M guaranteed): same Fit Score logic, higher stakes.
NBA
National Basketball Association

The NBA presents the most financially concentrated injury management problem in professional sports. A franchise pays a maximum salary player $50M–$60M per year. A single preventable injury to that player in a playoff run costs $2M–$5M in gate revenue, $10M–$30M in potential Finals revenue, and $50M–$200M in long-term franchise valuation impact. The 82-game season creates the most acute load management challenge in sport — back-to-back-to-back schedules, altitude changes, time zone travel — all tracked in fragmented sports science systems today.

One star player injury from preventable load spike: $50M+ in playoff revenue at risk. HERMES's S2/S3 cross-domain signal layer justifies the platform cost on a single back-to-back decision.
MLB
Major League Baseball

162 games produces the highest volume load management problem in professional sports. Combined with Statcast integration for biomechanical signal extraction — spin rate decline, release point inconsistency, velocity trajectory — HERMES creates an injury prevention system with direct, measurable impact. Statcast-sourced velocity decline and release point shift are measurable early warning signals for UCL stress, detectable 4–8 weeks before a Tommy John injury.

Starting pitcher Tommy John surgery: 12–18 months lost production on a $15M–$30M/year contract, plus replacement cost. HERMES detects the pattern before the injury.

7 — The Program Opportunity
Section 7
The Program Opportunity

The opportunity for a Power 4 program is not to add another platform to a stack that already has 15 to 25 systems. The opportunity is to create a more intelligent program operating model — one in which athletes receive more coordinated support, staff spend less time on manual reconciliation, the athletic director has real-time program visibility, and institutional leadership makes compliance and financial decisions with confidence rather than latency.

HERMES is MetVero's strategic commitment to the institutions that deploy it: not as a vendor relationship, but as an institutional intelligence partnership. The goal is not to deploy software. The goal is to build the intelligence capabilities that allow a program to operate as a competitive, compliant, and financially efficient enterprise in an environment where fragmented information is the primary operational liability.

Why This Era Makes the Intelligence Layer Urgent

Three structural shifts in college football have converged to make the intelligence layer a competitive necessity rather than a nice-to-have:

  • NIL complexity. 85 scholarship athletes × active NIL market = 50–65 simultaneous contracts with brand conflicts, booster entanglement risk, and expiration clocks. No legacy compliance system was built for this. Spreadsheets are not a compliance strategy.
  • Transfer portal velocity. The portal moves in 48-hour windows. Programs that cannot complete cross-domain due diligence faster than competitors consistently miss the players that change program trajectories. Decision speed is now roster construction strategy.
  • APR financial exposure. The NCAA's APR enforcement mechanism creates a direct link between academic support outcomes and bowl eligibility. For an SEC program, the gap between managing APR proactively and discovering a problem at annual reporting time can be $6M to $23M. No program should discover its bowl exposure from an NCAA report.

"The program already has the data. It already has the staff. It already has the systems. What it does not have is a layer that connects those systems into institutional intelligence. You are already paying for everything HERMES needs to work. You are not paying for the layer that makes it work together."

What HERMES Is Not

HERMES is not a system replacement project. It does not require programs to migrate off Catapult, Hudl, Banner, Teamworks, or any existing platform. It does not require staff to change the way they use the tools they already use. It sits above those systems, reads what they produce, and surfaces the intelligence that none of them can produce individually. The total lift required from the program's IT function is connection access — APIs, SFTP exports, scheduled reports. HERMES does the rest.

Appendix A — Data Exchange Specification
Appendix A
Data Exchange Specification

Purpose

This appendix defines what data may cross the program → HERMES boundary, subject to program approval per element during Discovery, at what sensitivity, for what purpose, on what cadence, and under what retention. The data inventory below defines the full pilot scope. The Phase 1 minimum-viable data set is a subset confirmed during Discovery.

Access Standards

Unless a section states otherwise: read-only access via supported reports, exports, APIs, or SFTP. No admin credentials, no raw database access, no write-back. Default retention: lifetime of engagement + 7 years. Source system object names in [brackets] are placeholders, replaced with actual report or endpoint names during system owner verification.

Out-of-Scope Across All Sources

HERMES does not consume any of the following from any source unless expressly approved in writing by the program and institutional legal counsel:

  • Social Security Numbers or date of birth
  • Financial aid records, scholarship dollar amounts, billing or account balances
  • Health records, medical records, mental health records, counseling records (beyond the three HIPAA clearance-only fields defined in A.4)
  • Parent or guardian information, emergency contact information
  • Disciplinary records, Title IX records
  • Free-text clinical notes of any kind
  • Any student records subject to a FERPA directory opt-out

Retention and Deletion

Default retention: lifetime of engagement + 7 years post-engagement, subject to institutional records management policy. Final retention schedule confirmed during Discovery. Upon written notice from the program, all consumed source data is deleted within 30 days. HERMES-derived data (risk scores, projections, pattern models) is deleted or anonymized at the program's election. Backup purge rotation: 90 days. Deletion certificates issued to the program upon completion.
